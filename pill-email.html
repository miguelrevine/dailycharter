Time Value of Money:
Present Value of a Perpetuity

A perpetuity pays a fixed amount forever. Because payments never end, you can't sum them one by one — but the geometric series collapses to a single division:

  PV = PMT / r

Two details the exam loves to test:

1.  The formula gives the value one period before the first payment.

2.  Preferred stock with a fixed dividend is the classic real-world perpetuity.

Pill 042 · Present Value of a Perpetuity. Here's why each option is there:

A. $27.00 The multiply trap: 4.50 × 0.06 = 0.27, shifted. If your answer is smaller than the payment, something's off — a perpetuity is worth many payments.
B. $75.00 ✓ PV = PMT / r = 4.50 / 0.06. Fixed-dividend preferred stock is valued exactly like a perpetuity.
C. $270.00 The decimal slip: dividing by 6 instead of 0.06 loses a factor of... wait, that gives 0.75. This one divides by 0.0166 — a rate misread. Always restate the rate as a decimal before you divide.
